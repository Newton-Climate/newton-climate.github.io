Welcome! I am a climate scientist and marathon runner in the 5th year of my Ph.D. at the California Institute of Technology.

My Research

I am a 5th year PhD Candidate at the Center for Global Climate Change at Caltech. I am currently being advised by Dr. Christian Frankenberg, whose lab specializes in carbon cycling and remote sensing. My focus is on understanding the ultimate fate of methane and other carbon species in the atmosphere. To do this, I apply remote sensing and data assimulation to improve the way we detect, quanitfy, and trace carbon with satellites and ground-based sensors.

Service

Cofounder: Systemic Access Mentorship Program

I cofounded this mentorship program to bring together blind students in STEM and provides tools for success. To date, there have been 40 matched participants/mentees across 3 countries.

Member: Caltech Graduate Admissions Policy Committee

I was selected by the President of Caltech to be student representative for the faculty committee tasked with increasing student body diversity and rewriting graduate admissions policies. This work involved extensive analysis of past admissions data and policies from other institutions and resulted in a published white paper and a newly implemented admissions protocol by the university. [Read the report]

Member: American Association of Physics Teachers Committee for Accessible Labs

As a member of this committee, I was tasked with making recommendations for university and k-12 physics lab accessibility. We published one white paper on this subject after thorough research on best practices for accessibility and education for disabled students. [Read the report]
